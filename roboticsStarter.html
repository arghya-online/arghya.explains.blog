All You Need to Know Before Building Your Bot

Beginner Friendly Checklist

Intro:

Phase 1: Major things you need to know to make your bot:

  • Computer Programming
  • Electronics
  • Mechanical Design

a. Computer Programming:

C/C++, python.

b. Electronics:

  1. Power System
  2. Control System
  3. Sensors (Input Devices)
  4. Actuators (Output Devices)
  5. Communication Systems
  6. Display & Feedback Systems
  7. Motor Drivers & Controllers

1. Power System

  • Batteries (Li-ion, LiPo, NiMH, Lead-Acid)
  • Voltage Regulators
  • Power Distribution Circuits
  • Solar Panels (for autonomous robots)

2. Control System

  • Microcontrollers (Arduino, ESP32, STM32, PIC)
  • Single-Board Computers (Raspberry Pi, Jetson Nano)
  • Robot Operating System (ROS) Controllers

3. Sensors (Input Devices)

  • Environmental Sensors
    • Temperature Sensors (DHT11, DS18B20)
    • Humidity Sensors (DHT22, AM2302)
    • Gas Sensors (MQ-2, MQ-135)
    • Light Sensors (LDR, TSL2561)
  • Motion & Position Sensors
    • Accelerometers (MPU6050, ADXL345)
    • Gyroscopes (MPU6050, L3G4200D)
    • IMU (MPU6050, BNO055)
    • Encoders (Rotary, Linear)
  • Proximity & Obstacle Detection Sensors
    • Ultrasonic Sensors (HC-SR04, MaxBotix)
    • Infrared Sensors (IR Proximity, TCRT5000)
    • LIDAR (RPLIDAR, Velodyne)
  • Vision Sensors
    • Cameras (ESP 32 Cam module, Pi Camera, OpenMV, Intel RealSense)

4. Actuators (Output Devices)

  • Motors (For movement & control)
    • DC Motors (Geared, Brushless, Coreless)
    • Servo Motors (MG995, SG90)
    • Stepper Motors (NEMA17, NEMA23)
    • Linear Actuators

5. Communication Systems

  • Wired Communication
    • UART (TX/RX Communication)
    • I2C (Inter-Integrated Circuit)
  • Wireless Communication
    • Bluetooth (HC-05, HM-10)
    • Wi-Fi (ESP8266, ESP32)
    • Radio Frequency (nRF24L01, LoRa)
    • GSM/GPS (SIM800L, NEO-6M)

6. Display & Feedback Systems

  • LCD Displays (16x2, 20x4)
  • OLED Displays (SSD1306, SH1106)
  • LED Indicators (RGB LEDs, Addressable LEDs - WS2812)
  • Haptic Feedback (Vibration Motors, Piezo Buzzers)

7. Motor Drivers & Controllers

  • H-Bridge Circuits (L298N, L293D)
  • ESC (Electronic Speed Controllers) for BLDC Motors
  • PWM Controllers (for motor speed control)
  • Servo Motor Drivers (PCA9685)
  • Stepper Motor Drivers (A4988, DRV8825)

c. Mechanical Design:

  • 1. Degrees of Freedom (DoF) – How a robot moves
    • Linear (Straight-line movement)
    • Rotational (Turning motion)
  • 2. Joints & Motion – How parts connect & move
    • Fixed (No movement)
    • Revolute (Rotates like a hinge)
    • Prismatic (Slides like a drawer)
  • 3. Robot Body & Structure – The Frame
    • Chassis (Main body)
    • Materials (Plastic, Metal, 3D-Printed)
    • Reinforcement techniques (weight distribution, durability)
  • 4. Wheels & Mobility – How Robots Move
    • 2-Wheel Drive (Common for small bots)
    • 4-Wheel Drive (Better stability)
    • Tank Tracks (For rough terrain)
  • 5. Gears & Transmission – Motion Transfer
    • Gears (Adjust speed/torque)
    • Belts & Chains (For mechanical linkages)
    • Actuators (Servos, stepper motors)

CAD Modeling & Simulation

  • 🔹 CAD Software for Robotics Design
    • Fusion 360 / SolidWorks (For 3D modeling)
    • TinkerCAD (Beginner-friendly 3D design)
    • AutoCAD (For detailed engineering drawings)
  • 🔹 Simulation Software for Testing
    • Proteus / TinkerCAD (For circuit simulations)
    • MATLAB Simulink (For advanced robotic modeling)
